Onestop Club

One Stop Club is a mobile app for finding dance events from sources like Resident Advisor, Facebook, and Eventbrite all in one place. It's available on both iOS and Android, following their design guidelines.

Users can discover, save, and add events to their calendar, as well as search for artists, clubs, and promoters. The app simplifies the search for alternative/underground nightlife.

User Flow

Following a competitor analysis of event apps on the market to assess the scope of the project, I quickly designed an original user flow with only 6 screens to conceptualize this small project in a minimal amount of time. Throughout the multiple iterations the tasks and number of screens was significantly refined, resulting in the final user flow seen here.

Defined Features

  • Aggregated events from across the internet
  • Skippable account setup with basic search function
  • Personalized local feed
  • Search function by date and location
  • Shopping bag preview page
  • Follow favorite artists, clubs and promoters

Low Fidelity Wireframe Sketch

With the user flow in mind and after reviewing the respective design guidelines for each platform I began designing some wireframe sketches for both versions of the app.

Mid-Fidelity Wireframes

Using iOS and Material Design component kits from the Figma community I translated the sketches into digital wireframes. Typography, typeface (SF Pro text under 20 and SF Pro Display above 20 for iOS; Roboto for Material Design) and a majority of components (using Figma community kits) follow iOS and Material Design guidelines as much as possible. Custom components were created when necessary.

Colors

The primary color scheme was inspired by Sanzo Wada’s color combination number 046 (A Dictionary of Color Combinations, 2010, p. 48). With the app being aimed at nightlife events the predominant use of dark colors seemed the most logical with orange offering a striking contrast.

Conclusion

What went well?

The app received positive feedback, especially on its color scheme and functionality. Focusing on a small-scale event app concept facilitated learning platform-specific guidelines.

What were the challenges?

Designing for Android as an iOS user required extra time to understand its guidelines. Differences between low and high fidelity wireframes were prominent in Android. Nonetheless, I've become more adept at using Material Design.

What could be improved?

More user testing is needed, especially for colorblind accessibility. Exploring alternative color schemes could enhance usability.
