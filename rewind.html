Rewind

Rewind is a Music Player app dedicated to 80s music. Users can listen to songs, sing along with scrolling lyrics, create profiles and playlists. It stands out with its 80s-inspired design, offering a unique experience compared to competitors.

Designed for iOS, users can also search for artists, use advanced search filters, and engage socially by commenting on playlists and following others.

responsibilities

UX Research, UX Design, UI Design

Show prototype

User Flow

After completing a competitor analysis and a small sample of interviews, I started by creating a user flow detailing the different steps users will take to accomplish their goals. 3 main functionalities were defined for this project (create a profile, search for new artists and make a playlist). Preliminary research helped outline three additional functionalities (view lyrics when listening to a song, advanced search function and socials).

Low to Mid-Fidelity Wireframes

I began with sketches, then transitioned to low fidelity wireframes in Figma considering the desired functionalities and user flow. Usability testing was done with friends.

While certain features, like the bottom navigation bar, aligned with common music app designs, the advanced search function required multiple iterations.

Mood Board

To establish the look and feel of the app I then created a mood board. The main inspiration was the “Memphis” design popular in the 80’s with its prominent use of geometric shapes and pastel shades.

Logo

The logo, inspired by a 1950s design by Josef Müller-Brockmann (Schule für Sprachtherapie in Graphic Design vol. 1, Jens Müller, 2021, p. 446), depicts a speaker surrounded by a rewind arrow. Given the app's 1980s music focus, the rewind imagery resonates, referencing the era's cassette culture. The logotype employs capitalized Poppins Black with an inverted "R" to emphasize the rewind theme.

Colors

To balance nostalgia with usability, I redesigned the color scheme, starting with a white “Memphis” background created in Illustrator featuring geometric shapes in pastel tones. This captured the “Memphis” essence without being overpowering.

The main UI palette complements this with a flat minimal design. A dominant lilac purple pairs well with the background, giving a retro feel. Complementary shades of black, off-white, and occasional yellow and lavender accents ensure clarity. Pure white text contrasts the purple backgrounds.

Typography

Poppins, a geometric sans-serif typeface, was chosen for its scalability and its popularity in the latter half of the 20th century. It offers a modern, less formal feel than serif fonts. As an open-source option from Google fonts, Poppins feels fresher than the commonly used 80s Helvetica.

Final Design

Conclusion

What went well?

The app’s unique look added significant personality. Establishing a robust visual identity early on paved the way for the final design.

What were the challenges?

Designing an advanced search function was time-consuming due to its novelty in music apps. Perfecting the color scheme to embody the Memphis design while ensuring usability also took multiple iterations.

What could be improved?

Implementing a dark mode could improve user experience during prolonged usage, aligning with standards in other music apps.
